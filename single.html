How to pull up "on the biceps" or why the biceps do not grow?

Today we have" on the menu " a secret chip that will help you make pull-UPS and pull-UPS with a narrow reverse grip effective for biceps growth.

Everyone for some reason thinks that the vertical pull-up and pull-up with a wide grip is a training of the widest back muscles, and pull-UPS and pull-UPS with a narrow reverse grip, or a grip "palms to yourself" is a biceps workout! I thought so myself as a teenager, until I read a book about training by Mike Mentzer. The famous bodybuilder believed that the pull with a narrow grip is the most effective exercise for developing a wide one backs.

In fact, it is not difficult to figure out what role the back plays in pull-UPS with a reverse grip, and what role the biceps plays. Perform 8 repetitions of pull-UPS to failure. So you'll figure it out how much weight can you lift with your back and biceps at the same time?

Then you will need to find out how much weight you can lift on the Scott bench, that is, in isolation, without the help of your back. After all, even in lifting the barbell standing on the biceps without cheating, a large role is played by the muscles of the back and press. Even imperceptibly swaying the body, we easily add 5 kg in the lifting on the biceps!

In the usual pull-UPS "on the biceps", the back takes up to 75% of the entire load!

As a rule, a person who is able to pull himself up with his own weight of 80 kg 8 repetitions "palms to yourself", lifts Scott on the bench in a strict technique of 25-30 kg. This results in more than 65-70% of the load in when doing pull-UPS for the biceps, it is not the biceps at all, and this exercise is not effective for arm training.

From the mathematical theory, I suggest moving on to the anatomy of muscles. The main function of the shoulder biceps is to bend the arm at the elbow joint. The main functions of the latissimus muscle are extension and adduction shoulder's. This means that any pull where our elbow joints are brought to the body, whether through the sides or in front, will involve just the function of the latissimus dorsi muscle, and the movement will no longer be produced mainly at the expense of the biceps.

From this we can draw the only possible conclusion: if our goal is to pump up the biceps with pull-UPS, then we must manage to minimize the reduction of the shoulder and maximize the flexion of the arm at the elbow joint.

And if we pull up, or do a block pull, pulling the bar or bar much lower than the chin, our elbows will inevitably spread out to the sides and they will be brought to the body, and the function of bending the arm will become secondary.

Therefore, we will need special, bicep pull-UPS or pull-UPS.

Movement technique for maximum development of the biceps

1. Grasp the crossbar (the handle for vertical traction) with the narrowest possible grip to reduce the spread of the elbows to the sides.

2. Exhale, bend your arms at the elbows, making the movement mainly due to the biceps.

3. Keep your elbows as close as possible to each other (ideally keep your elbows on the floor). elbows together), pull up the handle (horizontal bar bar) approximately to the level of the forehead or bridge of the nose.

4. While exhaling, return to the starting position.
