How long should the strength training program last? Theory and practice

How long should the strength training program last? Theory and practice

In this article, you will learn how many minutes a natural athlete should train for optimal results from their training process, and how much "chemical bodybuilder"should train.

It would seem that why you know what are the differences between the training of a chemist and a straight man? This is very important! This is the only way you can understand why you can't copy the training programs of some Champions, otherwise you can lose your muscles instead of growing them!

When I started my career as a natural athlete (out of 27 years of total experience I have been training naturally for about 20 years), then I trained for 3-4 hours. I studied 3 times a week, according to the method of Joe Weider. It was about 17-19 exercises per workout, and I just couldn't make it in less than 3-4 hours, no matter how hard I tried!

Three years later, the same trainer who recommended this program to me was surprised that we perform bench presses with a weight of only 60-70 kg. He was disappointed and said that he needed to raise much more! At the same time, this coach admitted that he himself performed one and a half years, only 2-3 exercises, and I reached 150 kg in the bench press in this short period of time.

That's how we newcomers are given advice that even the enemy would not wish! Soon I began to train more and more competently, including reducing the training to 7 exercises, and its duration to 90 minutes maximum!

Muscle strength and mass began to grow noticeably faster! I started to press lying down 100 kg for 8 repetitions, on the biceps I lifted 50 kg for 8 times without cheating, the arm completely without chemistry grew to 40 cm!

To increase rest between sets, but reduce your workout time even more I switched to 4 classes a week, and reduced the number of exercises to 5-6! Soon I have reaped 120 kg on 5 times! The arm became 43 cm, the bicep began to lift 60 by 8, the deadlift reached 200 kg! In each exercise, I performed 3-4 sets before failure, and did 6-8 repetitions at most.

That is, it became noticeable that muscle mass and strength are growing the faster the training volume is reduced. Then I met a trainer who was a fan of Mike Mentzer and his training, which consisted of just one heavyweight approach!

This fan told me that I would shake 200 kg lying down without chemistry if I cut my training even further. In his opinion, it was necessary to perform at all 1 super effective set of each exercise, and the entire workout should last at most 20 minutes!

As a Methodist, I of course "fit in", after which for the next 8 weeks I did not add even 1 kg in the bench press! Then I switched back to my system of 3-4 sets before failure, and added about 10 kg in the bench press for the next two months! By the way this program, which gave me such a great natural progress, I attach to this article in the form of a video (at the bottom).

Using this experimental method, I calculated the optimal volume of the muscle group for a natural one an athlete who made at least 3 sets and no more than 6 failure sets for each muscle group.

The fact is that when the training volume increases in the blood of an athlete, so-called catabolism hormones begin to be produced, especially cortisol, which leads to the loss of muscle mass, and the deterioration of fat burning. It is for for this reason, a "natural" person cannot combine running, martial arts and "rocking"classes!

The more we train in terms of increasing the amount of work we do, for example, the number of barbell lifts, the more our muscles burn up in the flames of catabolism. If the athlete uses pharmacological drugs medications of a hormonal nature, this reduces the level of catabolism in the body. There is an opportunity to eat less, and at the same time train more!

But, even a chemical athlete can bring himself up to overtraining, and losing muscle mass, or simply progressing more slowly, due to an excessive amount of training. One of the reasons why chemical athletes of the Golden era of bodybuilding, who are on anabolics, were so different from modern chemical athletes, is the reduction of training cycles volumes'.

Previously, it was quite normal to do 8.10 or 12 heavy sets of any exercise. Now rarely anyone performs more than 3-5 failure approaches, even on strong drugs.

Therefore, if even chemistry can not completely extinguish the flame of catabolism, then in natural bodybuilding it is better not to light it at all, by training briefly, intensely, and doing only the most important exercises. Do not" finish off the muscles", think about doing a couple more exercises to"exactly catch up". You just ruin your own progress!
